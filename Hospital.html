INSIGHTS AND FINDINGS

In-depth analysis of the dashboard reveals the following critical insights:

1. Revenue Trends: Revenue increased in 2022 but sharply declined in 2023, with Q3 2023 being particularly low.

2. Doctor and Patient Distribution: There is no specific breakdown by state, year, or gender in this view, but these filters are available to explore more granular insights.

3. Procedure Profitability: Heart Bypass Surgery, Skin Biopsy, and Knee Replacement have the highest profit margins, while procedures like MRI Scan have lower margins.

4. Specialty Performance: Dermatology, Radiology, and Pediatrics are the top revenue-generating categories.

DASHBOARD PREPARATION PROCESS

The following are the preparation process undertaken to generate this comprehensive analysis:

1. Data Collection: The data came from various hospital records, including patient visits, procedure billing, doctor performance, and financial transactions.

2. Data Cleaning: The Data was cleaned and standardized, ensuring consistency in fields like procedure names, dates, and revenue figures.

3. Data Integration: The cleaned data is then integrated into a centralized database or a data warehouse.

4. Data Modeling: Relationships between tables (e.g., Transactions, Procedures, Doctors) are established, often using SQL, to support the necessary analysis.

5. Dashboard Development: Power BI was used to create the dashboard, with the above SQL queries running in the background to populate the visualizations.

RECOMMENDATIONS

Based on the insights derived from the dashboard, the following recommendations are made:

1. Investigate Revenue Decline: The significant drop in revenue in 2023 should be analyzed. Factors such as changes in patient volume, pricing strategies, or external economic conditions might be affecting performance.

2. Optimize Low-Margin Procedures: Consider revising pricing or cost structures for procedures with lower profit margins like MRI Scans to improve overall profitability.

3. Enhance High-Revenue Specialties: Invest in marketing and resources for top-performing specialties like Dermatology and Radiology to maximize revenue potential.

CONCLUSIONS

The analysis reveals critical challenges and opportunities in the hospital's financial performance. The sharp revenue decline in 2023, particularly in Q3, signals an urgent need for a thorough investigation into potential causes, such as reduced patient volumes or pricing adjustments. While high-margin procedures and specialties like Dermatology and Radiology present growth opportunities, the profitability of low-margin procedures, such as MRI scans, needs to be addressed to optimize overall financial health. By focusing on these areas, the hospital can develop targeted strategies to reverse the revenue decline and enhance profitability across its service offerings.
